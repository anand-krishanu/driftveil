DriftVeil - AI Driven Machinery Drift Detection System | Business Plan (1/2)
Problem Description & Business Scenario : Manufacturing downtime costs $50B annually, and factories lose about $125,000 
per hour when machines fail. Most plants use threshold-based systems that alert only after damage starts.
Small daily changes (e.g., a motor rising 0.3°C/day) go unnoticed until sudden failure, this is the silent drift problem.
Problem Scope: Slow, hidden performance decline in machines like motors, pumps, conveyors, and bottling lines.
Multiple sensors degrade slightly together, but no single alarm triggers.
Target: While tailored for mid-size SME manufacturing lines, it natively scales to enterprise-level.
Target Users/Stakeholders: Plant Operators, Maintenance Engineers, Factory Owners, Production Managers

Solution Overview: DriftVeil is an AI platform that continuously calculates telemetry drift to identify hidden machine degradation before 
sudden failure. It utilizes What-If simulations to test corrective actions predicting exactly how specific machine adjustments will impact 
time-to-failure, long before SCADA threshold alarms ever trigger.
Technical Details: The MVP operates natively within an AWS environment, streaming TimescaleDB telemetry via secure, 
edge-encrypted OT network gateways. CUSUM algorithms and z-scores track the drift slope with executing live cross-machine 
correlation. Four specialized AWS Strands Agents (powered by Amazon Bedrock) autonomously process these metrics via a secure 
MCP-native architecture, grounded by a continuous fingerprint library feedback loop. After aggregating 6 months (~1TB) of 
operational data per facility, our continuous feedback loop will safely upgrade to Deep Learning LSTMs.
Innovation: Crucially, we augment, rather than replace, incumbent SCADA infrastructure from legacy vendors like Siemens and 
Rockwell. Driven by design thinking, our differentiator eliminates black-box AI by catching the continuous slope rather than just the 
traditional SCADA anomaly spike. Pipeline: [Live Telemetry] → [CUSUM Score] → [Bedrock Root-Cause Reasoning] → 
[Predictive ETA]. Bedrock Guardrails guarantee zero hallucinations.
Market Potential: Validated by McKinsey’s $8B Predictive Maintenance TAM, our scalable B2B SaaS architecture targets critical 
industry domains. Capturing an initial 2% SOM drives high recurring revenue. By preventing an $80K manufacturing stoppage with a $2K 
scheduled repair, clients immediately realize our validated 4,000% ROI for every early drift event caught.
Primary benefits: Early drift detection; prevention of sudden breakdowns; proactive maintenance; improved equipment reliability; 
explainable insights that improve decision support, reduced disruption to operations; higher uptime
Efficiency and flexibility: Real-time monitoring; continuous sensor-based analysis; adaptable across machine types; flexible 
deployment across plants; easy integration with industrial systems; no replacement of existing hardware
Time and cost saving: Reduced downtime; lower repair costs; fewer emergency interventions; optimized maintenance scheduling; 
reduced manual inspection effort; lower production loss; extended machine lifespan
Scalability: Multi-machine deployment; line-level scalability; plant-wide adoption; modular architecture; enterprise-ready monitoring 
framework; future expansion across industries
Social Impact: Safer industrial operations; reduced resource wastage; improved sustainability; support for smart manufacturing
